As an aspiring software engineer, I am drawn to the technology industry for its meritocratic nature. I have found that within technology, from start ups like Noble Manhattan where I worked as a Web Development Intern to even at large corporate organisations like HSBC where I was selected as a Head Start Technology Intern, there is often a refreshing emphasis on the quality and impact of one's work. This is incredibly motivating for me, as it means that the effort I put into honing my skills will be directly reflected in my success.

Secondly, coding has become an incredible tool for problem-solving, personally. When faced with real-world challenges, I now can create custom digital solutions.

For example, recently, I came across a Twitter account with over 400,000 followers that just posted daily exchange rates for the Nigerian Naira. Inspired by this, I decided to automate the process for the Angolan Kwanza by leveraging currency and Twitter APIs, along with CRON automation. The account gained traction, and I realised that just a year ago, this would have been beyond my capabilities. Now, I have the skills to build solutions to my own problems and those of others.

In summary, meritocracy and the ability to solve real-world problems through coding are the key reasons why I am excited to pursue an internship in technology. I am eager to make an impact, learn from experienced professionals, and grow as a software engineer within your organisation.
